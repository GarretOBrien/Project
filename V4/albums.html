In my Room

In late 2015, Collier began working on his debut album In My Room, He arranged, recorded and produced the album entirely on his own, playing every instrument, in addition to writing eight of the eleven songs. The album was recorded and mixed in one three-month period in the music room of his family home in London, England, hence the title

Hideaway-Jacob Collier

#IHarmU

In anticipation of the release of In My Room, Collier launched the "#IHarmU" campaign through Patreon, a crowdfunding site for content creators, where $100 patrons sent him 15-second video clips of melodies, which he harmonised with multiple vocal parts with his well-known multi-screen layout, and uploaded to his social media platforms.

Grammys

In February 2017, Collier won two Grammy Awards. Best Arrangement, Instrumental or A Cappella, for the Stevie Wonder song "You and I" and Best Arrangement, Instrumental and Vocals, for a cover of "Flintstones". All of these songs were from his debut album In My Room.

Djessie Vol.1

On the 29th of October, 2018, Jacob Collier announced a new, four-volume, 50-song musical project, titled Djesse. The first single of the Djesse project, "With The Love in My Heart", which would be released on the 2nd of November. Two further singles, "Ocean Wide, Canyon Deep" and "All Night Long", were released in late November. Djesse Vol. 1 was released in full on December 7th. The volume features collaborations with Voces8 , Laura Mvula,Hamid El Kasri, Take 6, and his mother, Suzie Collier. The Metropole Orkest featured heavily throughout the album, appearing on every track in addition to Collier's vocals and various instruments. Collier produced, arranged and orchestrated all the music himself.

Djesse Vol.2

The first single from Djesse Vol. 2, "Make Me Cry", was released on April 12th 2019. On 26 April 2019, Collier released the second single of the album, a cover of The Beatles' song "Here Comes The Sun" in collaboration with Dodie Clark , another internet musician. The third Vol. 2 single was released on June 13th 2019, an a cappella arrangement of "Moon River" by Henry Mancini and Johnny Mercer with over a hundred vocal contributions from family members, mentors, friends, and other Djesse collaborators. The fourth and final single from Djesse Vol. 2, "It Don't Matter" featuring vocals of American singer-songwriter JoJo was released on July 5th 2019.

Grammys Again!

On 20 November 2019, Djesse Vol. 1 and Vol. 2 each garnered a nomination for the 62nd Annual Grammy Awards, for "All Night Long" (feat. Take 6) and "Moon River" respectively. The nominated tracks both won their respective categories at the awards.

Djesse Vol.3

In November 2019, Collier announced the first single from Djesse Vol. 3, "Time Alone With You" in collaboration with Daniel Caesar, which was released on November 29th 2019. The video for the song premiered on YouTube on December 5th. In March 2020, Collier released the second single from Djesse Vol. 3, "In My Bones (feat. Kimbra & Tank and the Bangas)", premiering the music video on the same day on his YouTube channel. On 13 May 2020, on instagram Collier put out a post saying he would release a third single from the album if he received 12,000 comments on his post. His community reached this number and on May 14th the third single was released. This third single is "All I Need" and features Ty Dolla Sign and Mahalia.
